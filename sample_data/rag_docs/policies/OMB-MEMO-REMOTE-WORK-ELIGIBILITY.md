# Memorandum for Heads of Executive Departments and Agencies

**Subject:** Eligibility and documentation requirements for routine telework and remote work arrangements  
**From:** Office of Management and Budget — Workforce Policy Division *(synthetic sample)*  
**Date:** March 12, 2024  
**Classification:** Unclassified — Internal workforce management

## 1. Purpose

This memorandum establishes minimum documentation standards for approving routine telework and remote work for federal employees in positions that do not require daily physical presence at a government facility.

## 2. Eligibility criteria

An employee may be considered eligible when all of the following apply:

1. **Position suitability.** The position is designated as telework-eligible in the official position description and agency telework policy.
2. **Performance.** The employee’s most recent rating of record is at least “Fully Successful” or equivalent.
3. **Security and privacy.** The employee can perform duties without unacceptable risk to classified information, controlled unclassified information (CUI), or personally identifiable information (PII). Workspaces must meet agency minimums for physical and technical controls.
4. **Equipment.** The employee has agency-approved endpoint protection, VPN where required, and a documented alternate worksite address on file.

## 3. Required documentation

Supervisors must retain:

- A signed **telework agreement** identifying core hours, emergency recall expectations, and equipment accountability.
- A **risk acknowledgment** when the employee accesses CUI or law enforcement sensitive material from a non-federal facility.
- **Quarterly attestations** that the alternate worksite continues to meet privacy and safety requirements.

## 4. Revocation

Agencies may revoke telework privileges with written notice when security incidents occur, performance falls below standards, or operational needs require on-site presence.
